SAFETY GUIDE

DO'S

  • Use EPA-registered Insect Repellents: Apply products containing DEET, picaridin, IR3535, or oil of lemon eucalyptus to exposed skin. .
  • Wear Protective Clothing: Wear long-sleeved shirts, long pants, socks, and shoes, especially during peak mosquito activity (early morning and late afternoon)..
  • Use Mosquito Nets & Screens: Install screens on windows and doors. Use mosquito nets while sleeping, especially during the day..

DON'T

  • Don't Take Aspirin or NSAIDs Initially:Avoid aspirin, ibuprofen, and naproxen sodium until you have consulted a doctor to rule out dengue, as these can increase the risk of bleeding.
  • Don't Let Water Stagnate: Do not leave uncovered containers, coolers, or flower pots with water, as these are breeding grounds for Aedes mosquitoes.
  • Don't Wear Dark, Tight Clothing:Mosquitoes are attracted to dark colors, and tight clothing allows them to bite through easily.
  • Don't Travel While Sick:Avoid traveling to areas with active outbreaks if you are pregnant or have underlying conditions (like diabetes or heart disease).
